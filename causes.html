GENDER-BASED VIOLENCE

Gender-based violence (GBV) is still a significant problem for human rights everywhere in the globe. GBV affects one out of every three women and girls, regardless of class, race, age, sexual orientation, disability status, or culture.Frequently, violence against women is justified by gender stereotypes. Cultural standards frequently dictate that men should be assertive, in charge, and domineering while women should be submissive, meek, and dependent on men for support. These conventions may openly encourage a culture of abuse.We need to ensure that victims of violence and those at risk of violence have access to comprehensive support, including high-quality medical care, psychosocial aid, judicial and legal services, safe havens, and financial aid. By donating to this cause, you can help a women or child get a safer environment.

FEMALE GENITAL MUTILATION

FGM is a very harmful practice that can result in long-term medical and psychological problems as well as complications during the process such heavy bleeding, and even death.Some cultures support it as a way to restrain girls' sexuality or preserve their chastity. Others require FGM as a condition of marriage or inheriting property. Societies where the practice is most common frequently view it as a rite of passage for girls.We may fight to ensure their implementation and enforcement while also supporting the creation of laws and policies aimed at eliminating and prohibiting FGM. In addition, we assist in ensuring that survivors of FGM and girls at risk of the procedure have access to appropriate care while working with communities to change the societal norms that support the procedure. Your contribution will support Women4Women's efforts to put an end to FGM and shield additional girls and women from the practice.

GENDER EQUALITY

Women and girls still experience abuse and prejudice after decades of advancement. According to a sociological viewpoint, if gender disparity is to be further decreased than it has already been since the 1970s, a complex combination of cultural and structural variables must be addressed.Therefore, a sociological approach offers a number of policies and actions to address the structural and cultural causes that contribute to gender inequality in order to diminish it. We must lessen the socialization of boys and girls into traditional gender roles by parents and other adults. Dispute the popular and news media's gender bias. Aiming for gender equality benefits everyone in society, especially girls and women, and is essential as it can create peace for everyone. By donating, you are creating a world of difference in the fight against gender inequality.
